Hiring With Values: Building a Faith-Aligned Team

You do not hire skills onto a team. You hire people into a family that ships.

February 11, 2025 · 3 min read

Every founder learns the same expensive lesson: a brilliant hire with bad character will cost you more than they ever produce. Hiring with values is how a faith-driven founder builds a team that can be trusted with the mission, not just the tasks.

Character is the multiplier

Skills can be taught. Integrity, humility, and diligence are much harder to install later. Hire for the traits that shape a culture, and coach the competencies. A team of capable, honest people compounds; a team of talented, toxic ones detonates.

"Whoever walks in integrity walks securely, but whoever takes crooked paths will be found out."

Proverbs 10:9

Shared purpose, not forced conformity

Building a values-aligned team does not mean hiring only people who believe exactly what you believe — and depending on your context, it legally cannot. It means being clear about the mission and the way you do things, and hiring people who genuinely share the how: honesty, service, excellence, respect.

Practical steps

  • Write your values as behaviors, then interview for evidence of them.
  • Check references for character, not just capability.
  • Hire slow, and be brave enough to let the wrong fit go.

A team is one of the truest things a founder builds. Build it out of people you would be proud to have shaped.
