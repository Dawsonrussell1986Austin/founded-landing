Faith-Driven Women Entrepreneurs: Business, Calling, and Grit

The Proverbs 31 woman ran ventures, traded, and invested. Ambition and faith were never at odds.

June 10, 2025 · 3 min read

If you are one of the many faith-driven women entrepreneurs building something real, you have probably felt the pull of two lies at once: that ambition is unspiritual, and that faith is a limitation. Scripture says something braver — that God calls women to build, lead, and create, and equips them to do it well.

She considers a field and buys it

The famous Proverbs 31 woman is not a quiet homemaker in the corner — she evaluates real estate, runs a trading business, manages a team, and profits from her work. Competence and faith were never in tension. They were always meant to travel together.

"She considers a field and buys it; out of her earnings she plants a vineyard."

Proverbs 31:16

Calling over comparison

Comparison is a particular thief for women in business — the highlight reels, the "how does she do it all" pressure. Your calling is yours. It is not diminished by anyone else's, and it does not require you to be everything to everyone.

Finding your feet

  • Root your confidence in calling, not in applause or approval.
  • Find your people — other women who build and believe.
  • Lead as yourself. You do not have to imitate anyone to be taken seriously.

Build boldly. The kingdom has always been carried, in large part, by women who refused to shrink.

Begin tomorrow morning well.
